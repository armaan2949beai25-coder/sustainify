Journey of Women's Rights

1848

First women's rights convention held in Seneca Falls.

1920

Women gain voting rights in many leading nations.

1979

CEDAW adopted by the UN General Assembly.

Present

Ongoing global fight for true equality and empowerment.

Voices of Change

Malala Yousafzai

"One child, one teacher, one book and one pen can change the world."

Nobel laureate advocating for female education.

Kalpana Chawla

"The path from dreams to success does exist."

First woman of Indian origin in space, breaking barriers.

Marie Curie

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood."

First woman to win a Nobel Prize, pioneer in science.

Savitribai Phule

"Go, Get Education... Be self-reliant, be industrious."

India's first female teacher and social reformer.
