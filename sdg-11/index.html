What is SDG 11?

Understanding the core of Sustainable Cities and Communities.

The Importance of Urbanization

More than half of the world's population lives in cities. By 2050, that figure will have risen to 6.5 billion people—two-thirds of all humanity. Sustainable development cannot be achieved without significantly transforming the way we build and manage our urban spaces.

The rapid growth of cities—a result of rising populations and increasing migration—has led to a boom in mega-cities, especially in the developing world, and slums are becoming a more significant feature of urban life.

The Goal

Making cities sustainable means creating career and business opportunities, safe and affordable housing, and building resilient societies and economies. It involves investment in public transport, creating green public spaces, and improving urban planning and management in participatory and inclusive ways.

Causes of Urban Issues

The primary challenges facing our cities today.

📈

Rapid Population Growth

Unplanned urban sprawl puts immense pressure on freshwater supplies, sewage, the living environment, and public health.

🚗

Traffic Congestion

Heavy reliance on private vehicles leads to severe traffic, increased greenhouse gas emissions, and poor air quality.

🏚️

Inadequate Housing

The lack of affordable housing options leads to the expansion of informal settlements and slums with poor infrastructure.

Solutions & Actions

How we can build better, more sustainable cities.

🚈

Eco-Friendly Transport

Investing in reliable, affordable, and sustainable public transportation systems like metros, trams, and electric buses.

🌲

Green Infrastructure

Developing urban forests, public parks, and green roofs to improve air quality and provide recreational spaces.

♻️

Circular Economy

Implementing strict waste management policies focused on reducing, reusing, and recycling municipal waste.
