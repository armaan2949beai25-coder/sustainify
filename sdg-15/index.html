Discover Our Mission

Biodiversity Conservation

Nature is critical to our survival. It provides us with our oxygen, regulates our weather patterns, pollinates our crops, produces our food, feed, and fibre. But it is under unprecedented stress.

Human activity has altered almost 75 per cent of the earth's surface, squeezing wildlife and nature into an ever-smaller corner of the planet. Around 1 million animal and plant species are threatened with extinction – many within decades.

Key Threats to Ecosystems

🌲

Deforestation

Forests are cleared for agricultural expansion, logging, and infrastructure, destroying critical habitats and releasing stored carbon.

🏜️

Desertification

Driven by climate change and unsustainable land use, fertile land turns into desert, threatening global food security and livelihoods.

🐘

Wildlife Poaching

Illegal wildlife trade and poaching drive many keystone species to the brink of extinction, disrupting delicate ecosystems.

Sustainable Solutions & Actions

Reforestation

Planting trees helps restore habitats, prevent soil erosion, and combat climate change by sequestering carbon.

Sustainable Products

Choose products certified by organizations like the FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) to ensure ethical sourcing.

Dietary Choices

Animal agriculture is a leading cause of deforestation. Reducing meat consumption directly lowers your environmental impact.

Protect Wildlife

Avoid products made from endangered species, such as ivory, rhino horn, or exotic leathers, and support conservation reserves.

Interactive Ecosystems

Forests

Importance: Home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity; crucial for carbon capture.

Threats: Logging, agricultural expansion, wildfires.

Conservation: Sustainable forestry, reforestation, protected areas.

Grasslands

Importance: Essential for grazing wildlife and livestock; significant carbon sinks.

Threats: Overgrazing, conversion to farmland, urban development.

Conservation: Rotational grazing, habitat restoration, land management.

Mountains

Importance: Provide freshwater to half the global population; harbor unique species.

Threats: Climate change, unsustainable tourism, mining.

Conservation: Eco-tourism, sustainable resource extraction, climate action.

Wetlands

Importance: Natural water filters, flood protection, and highly productive habitats.

Threats: Drainage for agriculture, pollution, infrastructure development.

Conservation: Wetland restoration, pollution control, strict land use policies.

Deserts

Importance: Unique ecosystems with highly adapted flora and fauna.

Threats: Desertification, climate change, unsustainable groundwater use.

Conservation: Combating desertification, sustainable water management.
