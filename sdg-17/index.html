About SDG 17

Sustainable Development Goal 17 recognizes that a successful sustainable development agenda requires inclusive partnerships—at the global, regional, national, and local levels—built upon principles and values, a shared vision, and shared goals.

Sustainable Financing

Official Development Assistance remains critical for developing countries to overcome structural challenges.

Global Challenges

Addressing the critical barriers that prevent international cooperation and equitable development.

Funding Gaps

Developing nations lack financial resources to implement large-scale sustainable programs effectively.

Digital Divide

Unequal access to the internet and modern technologies isolates communities from global progress.

Unfair Trade

Trade barriers and disproportionate market access consistently hinder developing economies.

Data Discrepancies

A lack of reliable statistics restricts the ability to track progress and allocate resources properly.

Cooperative Solutions

Actionable strategies designed to foster international collaboration.

Assist developing countries in attaining long-term debt sustainability through coordinated policies aimed at fostering debt financing, debt relief, and debt restructuring.

Enhance North-South, South-South, and triangular regional and international cooperation on science, technology, and innovation access.

Increase international support for implementing effective and targeted capacity-building in developing countries to achieve national plans.

Encourage and promote effective public, public-private, and civil society partnerships, building on the experience and resourcing strategies of partnerships.
