The 2030 Vision

The UN's bold target is to eradicate all forms of hunger by 2030, ensuring everyone has access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round.

Root Causes of Hunger

📉 Poverty & Inequality +

Poverty is the primary driver of hunger. Families living below the poverty line cannot afford nutritious food, nor can they afford the seeds, tools, and land needed to grow their own. This creates a devastating cycle where hunger prevents people from working and learning, keeping them trapped in poverty.

🌍 Climate Change & Weather +

Changing weather patterns, severe droughts, and unprecedented floods destroy crops and livestock. Small-scale farmers are particularly vulnerable. When harvests fail, local food supplies plummet, prices skyrocket, and the poorest families are left unable to eat.

🗑️ Global Food Waste +

Shockingly, nearly one-third of all food produced for human consumption is lost or wasted globally. This happens due to poor harvesting techniques, lack of storage facilities in developing nations, and excessive consumer waste in developed countries. Reducing this waste could feed millions.

⚔️ Conflict & Instability +

Over half of the world's undernourished people live in conflict zones. War destroys agricultural infrastructure, displaces farming families, and disrupts trade routes. In these areas, food is often weaponized, leading to severe man-made famines.
