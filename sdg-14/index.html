The Heart of Our Planet

Oceans drive global systems that make the Earth habitable for humankind. Our climate, weather, and even the oxygen we breathe are regulated by the sea.

70%

Of the Earth's surface is covered by oceans, containing 97% of the planet's water.

3 Billion

People depend on marine and coastal biodiversity for their livelihoods globally.

30%

Of CO2 produced by humans is absorbed by oceans, buffering global warming impacts.

Critical Threats to Marine Life

Despite their importance, our oceans are facing unprecedented ecological crises.

Marine Pollution

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter our oceans annually, creating vast garbage patches and endangering thousands of marine species through ingestion and entanglement.

Ocean Acidification

Increased carbon dioxide absorption alters ocean chemistry. This acidification severely weakens coral skeletons and disrupts the development of shellfish.

Overfishing

Unsustainable fishing practices are depleting fish stocks faster than they can replenish, destabilizing entire marine food webs and ecosystems.

Coral Bleaching

Rising sea temperatures cause corals to expel their symbiotic algae. Without immediate climate action, 90% of coral reefs may disappear by 2050.

Turning the Tide

Global collaboration and individual actions are essential to restore marine ecosystems.

Protect Marine Biodiversity

Establish and expand Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) to safeguard critical habitats and allow endangered species populations to recover.

Sustainable Fishing

Implement strict science-based quotas, eliminate destructive fishing subsidies, and promote sustainable aquaculture practices.

Combat Plastic Pollution

Transition to circular economies, enforce single-use plastic bans, and innovate sustainable biodegradable alternatives.

Climate Action Integration

Aggressively reduce global greenhouse gas emissions to mitigate ocean warming, deoxygenation, and acidification.
